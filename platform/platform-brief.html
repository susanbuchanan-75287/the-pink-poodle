The Pink Poodle · Board Brief

Scaling to a multi-groomer platform

Should each groomer get their own 4-page website for $40/month? Architecture, unit economics, billing, and the legal reality — plus a working generator we built to prove the core.

Prepared for Susan Buchanan · Owner Status: strategy + MVP
TL;DR. The website half of this product is easy and cheap — we built a working generator that spins up a themed 4-page site from a JSON file (see the platform/ folder and the Happy Tails demo). The hard, expensive, risky half is everything behind it: subscription billing, a tenant-isolated backend, SMS at scale, per-state money/tax law, and support. At $40/mo the margin is real but thin once texting and support are honest. Recommendation: launch a small, deliberately-scoped pilot (5–10 local groomers), price at $49–$59/mo, and treat payments/SMS as the gating risks — not the website.

1. What the customer actually gets for $40

The pitch: a groomer signs up, and within minutes has a polished website + online booking, without touching code. Concretely, a tenant bundle would include:

Included (the promise)

  • Branded 4-page site — Home, Services, About, Book (built & demoed today)
  • Online booking form → their phone/inbox
  • Their own subdomain (name.groovepets.app) + optional custom domain
  • SMS/email confirmations & reminders
  • Mobile staff console (the spa app: board, checkout, clients)
  • Retail/inventory + reports + reschedule/waitlist (all built this month)

The catch (what it really costs us)

  • Every SMS costs money and needs 10DLC registration per brand
  • Taking their clients' card payments drags in money-transmitter law
  • Multi-state = sales-tax nexus + privacy-law patchwork
  • Support for non-technical owners is the real cost, not servers
  • Uptime/DR obligations rise once other people's revenue depends on us

2. Architecture — two paths

ApproachHowProsConsFit
A. Site-only, bring-your-own-ops Generate the 4-page site per tenant (done). Booking goes to their phone/email. No shared backend, no payments. Cheap, near-zero liability, ship this week. Almost pure margin at $40. Not "software" — it's a site + form. Easy for competitors/Wix to match. Pilot now
B. True multi-tenant SaaS One shared backend; every record keyed by tenantId; subscription billing; provisioning; per-tenant SMS brands & payments. Real product, real moat, real retention. This is what MoeGo/Gingr are. Months of work; ongoing compliance, support, and on-call. $40 may not cover it. Phase 2

The current app is single-tenant (collections like pp_spa_tickets assume one salon). Path B requires refactoring every collection and query to be tenant-scoped, plus a hard isolation boundary — the #1 security requirement from the earlier scaling brief.

What we built today (proves Path A + the site half of Path B)

A zero-dependency Node site generator (platform/site-generator/generate.js): one tenant JSON → a themed, responsive, SEO-ready 4-page site with a working booking form (POSTs to a configurable endpoint, falls back to SMS/mailto), honeypot spam guard, and schema.org structured data. The bundled Happy Tails Grooming tenant demonstrates a completely different brand from the same template.

Run: node generate.js --all → output in site-generator/sites/<slug>/. Deterministic and disposable.

4. Subscription billing (Stripe)

  • Stripe Billing with a monthly Price; Checkout for signup, Customer Portal for card updates/cancel (offloads PCI + dunning to Stripe).
  • A stripeCustomerId + subscriptionStatus on each tenant; a webhook (customer.subscription.updated/deleted, invoice.payment_failed) flips the tenant active flag.
  • Inactive/past-due tenant → site shows a soft "temporarily unavailable" and staff console is locked, but data is retained for a grace period.
  • Note: charging tenants a subscription (Stripe) is unrelated to their clients paying them — the latter is where money-transmitter law bites (§6).

5. Provisioning & domains

  1. Tenant completes Stripe Checkout → webhook creates the tenant record.
  2. Generator runs with their config → site files produced.
  3. Deploy to a per-tenant path/subdomain; issue SSL (wildcard cert for *.groovepets.app covers all subdomains automatically).
  4. Optional custom domain: tenant points a CNAME; we provision SSL on demand.
  5. An admin editor lets the owner tweak copy/colors/services → regenerate.

For a pilot, steps 2–3 can be semi-manual (you run the generator, drop files, add the DNS record) before automating.

6. The legal reality (recap of prior briefs — this is the gating risk)

Money movement highest risk

If clients pay through your platform and you hold/route funds, several states treat that as money transmission (licensing, bonds). Avoid by using Stripe Connect / Square so funds flow tenant↔processor directly and you never take custody. Your existing paymentCompliance pattern (block restrictive states until cleared) applies here.

SMS / TCPA / 10DLC operational

Each tenant sending texts needs a registered 10DLC brand + campaign and documented opt-in. Reminders are transactional (lower risk); any marketing blast is strictly opt-in. Budget registration time and per-tenant fees.

Multi-state tax accounting

Selling SaaS across states triggers economic-nexus sales tax in many jurisdictions. Use a tax engine (Stripe Tax) from day one. SaaS is taxable in some states, not others.

Privacy & SOC 2 customer-driven

SOC 2 is not legally required — it's a sales asset once you court bigger clients. State privacy laws (CA/CO/etc.) and a clear DPA/privacy policy matter more early. Hard tenant data isolation is the technical must-have.

8. Risks & what could sink it

  • High Support load — non-technical owners will call. This is the true cost and the margin killer. Cap it with great onboarding, templates, and self-serve editing.
  • High Payments/compliance — never take custody of funds; use Connect/Square; gate restrictive states.
  • Med Shared-fate reliability — an outage now hurts every tenant's bookings. Needs the DR runbook (still outstanding) before Path B.
  • Med Churn — solo groomers come and go; a dead tenant still costs support & a domain.
  • Low Tech to generate sites — solved. The easy part.

9. Recommendation & phased roadmap

Phase 0 — now (done): Site generator + Happy Tails demo prove the core. No spend, no liability.

Phase 1 — pilot (weeks): Hand-onboard 5–10 local groomers on Path A (site + booking-to-phone). Price $49/mo. Manual provisioning. Goal: learn the support reality and whether they'll pay. Add Stripe Billing for the subscription only.

Phase 2 — SaaS (months, only if Phase 1 retains): Refactor the backend to be tenantId-scoped with hard isolation; add Stripe Connect for tenant client-payments; 10DLC per tenant; Stripe Tax; self-serve editor + automated provisioning; DR runbook. Tiered pricing ($40 site / $79 full).

Do not build Phase 2 before Phase 1 proves people pay and quantifies support cost.

Board round verdicts

Product: Real niche (done-for-you, grooming-native, solo/small). Ship a pilot. ✅
Finance: $40 fine for site-only; raise to $49–$59 for full SaaS — support, not servers, sets the floor. ⚠️
Legal: Two hard rules — never custody funds (Connect/Square), and 10DLC + opt-in per tenant. Gate restrictive states as we already do. ⚠️
Engineering: Website generation solved today. Tenant isolation + billing + DR are the Phase-2 lift; don't start until the pilot validates demand. ✅/⚠️
Consensus: Green-light a 5–10 salon pilot at $49/mo on Path A; revisit full SaaS after 90 days of real support data. 🟢

This is a strategy brief, not legal, tax, or accounting advice. Confirm money-transmitter, sales-tax nexus, and TCPA/10DLC specifics with qualified counsel and a CPA before charging tenants or moving client funds. Built alongside the platform/site-generator MVP and the Happy Tails demo tenant.
