1. Team & PINs — who can do what

Everyone signs into the staff app with a PIN. There are three roles, ranked owner ▸ manager ▸ stylist:

RoleCan do
StylistThe board, check-ins, notifying owners, checkout, clients, vaccinations.
ManagerAll that, plus void paid tickets, edit fees & the ledger, and edit the required-vaccine list.
OwnerEverything, including the setup below.

Setting each person's PIN

Go to ⚙️ Fees & PIN → Employee PINs 👥. For each team member you can set their personal PIN and choose their role. There's a 🎲 Random PIN generator in the Salon Console staff editor that creates a one-time PIN you can copy and hand to them.

  • The shared salon PIN (Change owner PIN) always signs in as owner — a good fallback, but give people their own PINs so actions are traceable.
  • PINs are 4–8 digits, stored encrypted (we never show them again), and can't duplicate someone else's or the shared PIN.
Rotate a PIN whenever someone leaves or a device is lost. Clearing a person's PIN stops them signing in with it.

2. Owner notifications 📣

Once a pup is checked in, every board ticket shows buttons to notify the owner. This keeps people informed and cuts down on "is she ready yet?" calls.

ChannelStatusNotes
✉️ Email (checked in / ready)LiveSends instantly to the owner's email. Works any time we have their email on file.
🔔 Push (ready for pickup)LiveA notification on the owner's phone — but only if they opted in on their tracking page (see below).
💬 Text (SMS)Coming soonThe button is there but dormant. Turning on real texting requires a registered business texting number (US A2P 10DLC) — a future step.
How push opt-in works: after booking, the owner opens their tracking page (their booking code) and taps "🔔 Alert me when ready." Their phone is then linked to that one booking. The ticket shows a 🔔 alerts on badge so staff know push will reach them. No opt-in = staff simply use email.

Emails come from the salon's verified sender and are logged on each ticket, so you can see what was sent.

3. How customers book & track

  1. On the public site a customer fills in their pup, services, preferred time, and contact info, agrees to the vaccination/handling OKs, and taps Request.
  2. They get a 6-character booking code (REF). They enter that on the Track tab to watch the pipeline live — and to turn on pickup alerts.
  3. Fully booked? Customers can join the Waitlist; you text them when a slot opens.

Every booking automatically creates/updates the owner & pet in your Clients CRM — no double entry.

4. Fees, deposits & the review booster

All under ⚙️ Fees & PIN:

  • Fee list — the line items that appear at checkout. Edit prices any time (manager+).
  • Booking deposits — turn on to request a deposit (with a default amount) for appointments; it's applied at checkout.
  • Review booster — a few hours after pickup, automatically invite happy owners to leave a Google or Facebook review. Set it on/off, the delay, your review links, and the message. Off until you set it up.

5. Required vaccinations 💉

Under ⚙️ Fees & PIN set the required-vaccine list (manager+). Owners can upload proof at booking; staff verify or reject it on the board, and verified rabies dates save to the pet's profile. The 💉 Vaccinations tab shows who's due or missing proof.

A pup with no valid proof shouldn't be groomed — the app flags this clearly at drop-off.

6. The books — Ledger & Reports 📒

  • 📒 Ledger — every checkout posts a balanced double-entry journal automatically (debits always equal credits). Managers can add manual entries. Export to CSV any time.
  • 📊 Reports — sales and activity summaries.
  • Voids — a paid ticket is never deleted; voiding it (manager+) reverses the entry so your books stay accurate.
Because the ledger balances on every sale, your totals are always audit-ready. Export the CSV for your bookkeeper.

7. Messaging & promos

From the Clients and Messages areas you can reach customers three ways:

  • Email blast — to all customers, BCC'd so addresses stay private.
  • Web push blast — to everyone who subscribed to salon alerts on the public site (shows a live subscriber count).
  • SMS — real texting is a future step (needs the registered texting number). Until then, per-customer buttons open your own phone's Messages/Mail app pre-filled to send.
Opt-outs are honored automatically. Customers who reply STOP are unsubscribed; each has an opt-in flag in the CRM. Don't send marketing texts through the future SMS path until the number is properly registered.

8. Square (optional)

If you connect Square, the customer editor gains a Sync to Square button and each visit can be recorded as a sale in Square's history. It's optional and clearly labeled — if Square can't complete something, the app tells you instead of failing silently. Money never flows through this app; Square handles payments on your own account.

9. Security & privacy 🔒

  • Customer contact and pet info sits behind the staff PIN. Fine for a trusted front desk — rotate the PIN if a device is lost.
  • Nothing is stored in the browser: staff re-enter their PIN after a reload, and customers track by code (no saved login to steal).
  • The public booking form is rate-limited and spam-protected; owner emails come from a verified sender.
  • These manuals and the staff app are marked private (not listed on search engines).
